What is an anxiety attack?

An anxiety attack is a sudden feeling of fear of danger or discomfort created in your mind. You can notice a feeling of pressure over your chest, throat or around your upper body, but it's different for everyone. When you notice the anxiety, click on our "exercises" to get some help through it. Remember, you are all right and this will pass. Anxiety is difficult because it's so personal. Anxiety usually stems from trauma or something difficult in your personal life. What's important to know is that You are not alone to experience these feelings. Important things to know is that whatever feeling you're having can be hightened by the use of alcohol or drugs, things that amplify what you're already struggling with inside of you. So a good advice for you to get on top of your anxiety is to not use anything that alters your personality, lowers any barriers or gives you the possibility to escape. You can go right through the anxiety attack, even if it doesn't seem like it. You can. When it happens Anxiety buddy is here to help you. Please visit www.WHO.com to access more about mental health.
